The fuel system delivers fuel to the engine. Older bikes may use carburetors to mix air and fuel, while modern motorcycles mostly employ electronic fuel injection (EFI) systems for precise fuel delivery. The ignition system includes a spark plug and controls the timing of the spark to ignite the air-fuel mixture.
To ensure smooth operation and longevity, the engine has a lubrication system with an oil pump circulating oil to lubricate moving parts. An oil filter removes impurities from the oil. The electrical system includes a battery for starting and powering lights and an alternator/generator for charging the battery and supplying power when the engine is running.
